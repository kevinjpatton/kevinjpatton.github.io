Christine Korsgaard

The Deontologist

A leading contemporary philosopher, Christine Korsgaard builds upon the ethical theories of Immanuel Kant. Her approach to deontology centers on the concept of "practical reason" and autonomy.

When you encounter Korsgaard in our games, she won't care about the final score or the consequences of your actions. Instead, she will evaluate your intentions. She asks whether the principle behind your action is something you could logically will everyone else to do in the same situation.

"To value humanity is to see that our capacity to determine our own ends gives us a profound, unconditional worth."

Her Gameplay Advice: "Never treat another person merely as a means to an end. Respect their autonomy, and act only on rules that are fair for everyone to follow."

Jeremy Bentham

The Utilitarian

Jeremy Bentham is the founding father of modern utilitarianism. He viewed human beings as fundamentally driven by two masters: pain and pleasure.

In our simulations, Bentham acts as your moral accountant. He uses his "hedonistic calculus" to weigh the exact amount of happiness an action produces against the suffering it causes. To Bentham, a good action is simply a math problem where the net positive outcome is higher than any alternative.

"It is the greatest happiness of the greatest number that is the measure of right and wrong."

His Gameplay Advice: "Look at the big picture. Who wins? Who loses? Choose the path that creates the largest net positive for the entire system, regardless of traditional rules."

Thomas Aquinas

The Virtue Theorist

A towering figure in medieval philosophy, Thomas Aquinas integrated Aristotelian virtue ethics with Christian theology, forming the basis of Natural Law theory.

When Aquinas evaluates your choices, he is looking at how your actions shape your character. He focuses on the development of cardinal virtues (prudence, justice, fortitude, and temperance) and whether your choices align with the natural purpose of human life: ultimate flourishing.

"Good is to be done and pursued, and evil is to be avoided. All other precepts of the natural law are based upon this."

His Gameplay Advice: "Do not merely ask 'What should I do?' but rather 'What kind of person am I becoming?' Strive for excellence in character and harmony with the natural order."

Socrates

The Questioner

The classical Greek philosopher whose methods laid the groundwork for Western logic and philosophy. Socrates wrote nothing down, but his relentless questioning of his fellow citizens in Athens changed history.

Socrates won't tell you the right answer in our scenarios. Instead, he will appear to cross-examine your assumptions. Using the Socratic method (elenchus), he will point out the logical inconsistencies in your choices until you admit what you do not know.

"The unexamined life is not worth living."

His Gameplay Advice: "Define your terms. Why do you believe what you believe? Are you certain your principles hold up under scrutiny, or are you just following the crowd?"
