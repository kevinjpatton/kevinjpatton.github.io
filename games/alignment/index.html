Why is fixing AI so incredibly difficult?

1. AI Doesn't Understand "Fair"

To a computer math equation, saving 10 lives while ending 1 life is a net positive score of +9. The AI doesn't feel the moral weight of sacrificing the 1. We have to force AI to care about equity and fairness, because math naturally favors the majority.

2. The Hidden Cost of Optimization

Whenever an AI makes a system perfectly efficient (like automating all jobs or taking all the water), it usually destroys the "buffer room" that protects vulnerable people. Maximum efficiency almost always creates human suffering.

3. Who Gets to Decide?

When you moved those sliders, you were acting as the programmer. AI is not neutral. The programmers who build AI are secretly embedding their own personal values into the code. If a programmer values efficiency over safety, the whole world has to live with that choice.
